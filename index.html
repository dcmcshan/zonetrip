Working white paper | July 2026

A local AI mirror for community self-understanding.

Zone Trip is a non-directive civic reflection system. It helps a community encounter its own expressed tensions, contradictions, absences, and questions without turning reflection into governance, surveillance, polling, therapy, or behavioral direction.

Read the white paper Open listening booth Review deployment gates

Core question

Can a community be helped to see itself without that act of reflection becoming a way to direct its behavior?

Constitutional limits

The mirror must be weaker than the community.

The white paper frames usefulness as a risk. Zone Trip should refuse dashboards, rankings, recommendations, faction maps, retained raw datasets, and any host request that turns reflection into leverage.

Contained system

A narrow local data path.

The system is designed around local capture, transient interpretation, derived world-model updates, constitutional filtering, human steward review, and final public reflection.

    Refusal criteria

    Some communities should not have Zone Trip.

    Deployment is justified only where ordinary reflection is dispersed, obscured, or hard to hold collectively, and only where safeguards can be maintained without compromise.
